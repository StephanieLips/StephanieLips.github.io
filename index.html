Work-Life-Balance

Why bore-out is a thing

All of the time people wonder how I can cope with stress that well. I love stress and I love having a shitload of work to do. It's just how I am. And when you take the stress away from me I get bored quite easily. I have never had not much to nothing to do. But now it's the case and here is what this is doing to me:

1. It's stressing me out. But wait... didn't I just say that I don't have anything to do. Yes I did. But it's still stressful. You know that you have to be at work for 40 hours. You know that you have to do something at least. You know that you can't just book the next flight to somewhere or look into the best restaurants around you. You know that you sit there and just stare at the display trying to figure out which pages aren't suspicious to look at. And when other people walk by my work space I want to look like I have something to do and I'm busy.

2. It's making me depressed. Having to deal with depression anyways it's even harder to have a job which doesn't fulfill you. I always think about a thousand things which I could accomplish in that moment. It makes me feel worthless in that sense that I can't contribute anything to my company, my customer's satisfaction or even to myself. It is even proven that such a thing as a boreout is a medical condition as well as a burnout. And it makes you sick and even depressed (like I figured myself).

3. Beside of the physical and psychological effects on you it's even harder to explain this to your friends. All of them do have a lot or at least enough to do at work which makes them say things like "wow, be glad you have some free time" or "Don't complain. Could be worse". Of course they don't mean to harm you or belittle you in any way but it makes you doubt the gravity of your problem.

Summary: Please do not underestimate the power of boredom. It harms you in a lot more ways than you might think. I really have to reflect my way of living and working. I'll keep you posted what's going on and how I'll solve this.

Diet

Two weeks update

Now - two weeks in I wanted to give a review on what went well and what didn't. First of all I'm really happy that my friends and family got along with it very well.

I might just start with the downsides of being vegetarian. There are only two things I really recognized though. First of all some people react weird to me saying that I want to try a vegetarian lifestyle before fully adopting it. Yes, I get that it sounds weird but since changes are never easy and I actually liked the taste of meat I want to try it first. Secondly, I found it hard while eating out to spot the vegetarian dishes. I eat nearly everything so it shouldn't be hard, right? Wrong. But mostly a salad is vegetarian and most of the restaurants have salads. Helps me losing weight as well I guess.

Since there are always two sides to a matter, I want to talk about the good parts of vegetarianism as well. To get directly to the point: I feel great. I always struggled with an iron deficiency and I have the feeling that it's a problem that came back again. But there's not much you can't cure with some pills. Besides that I feel fitter and willing to do something and move my ass. My digestive system finally works a lot better (I'm not going into detail there). I pointed out in my downsides that some restaurants don't have a lot of vegetarian options. But I have to say that that's a good thing as well since I'm not that good in deciding what I want to eat.

I'm now really excited to continue this "experiment" and tell you after a month (or more) how I feel and what changed. Oh by the way... I didn't even eat fish last week. So I got that going for me which is nice.

Diet

One month vegetarian

Yes, I eat meat. Yes, I like it. Yes, I eat it without thinking about it. And that was the problem. I didn't think about what I put into my body. So I planned to eat only vegetarian food for a whole month. Besides that, I've read that you can create a habit after 30 days of doing something. So let's try.

I started out harder than I thought. I'm used to put meat in my meals without even recognizing it. Every time I made myself pasta or a salad, I put ham cubes in it. Since it was so common for me, it was especially hard to quit meat completely. But I started on Sunday - 02.09.2018 - and failed on the third day. Most of the time I take something to eat with me to work. On the fail-day, I wanted to have some vegetarian dumplings. So I got them and packed a little salad with vegetarian German ravioli and - without thinking about it - swizz meat salad (meat with cheese). When me and my collegues were back at work and starting to have lunch together I recognized that now there was meat in my lunch. I really hate throwing food away. That's why I ate it.

From that lunch on I managed to stay a vegetarian. It helps a lot to write a plan of what I will eat the next few days. There are some rules I made for myself you could judge me for. I'll still eat candy with gelatin and I'll still eat fish. That technically makes me a pescetarian. I want to try to eat as less as I can do of both.

I'm nearly done with my first week of being vegetarian. So far so failed. But I only can do better. I'll keep you posted.
